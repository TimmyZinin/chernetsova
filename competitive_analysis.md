Accounting for admin, notes, and preparation, the effective billable ratio is approximately 60–70% of working hours.

**Client lifecycle:** Average therapy engagement runs **6–12 sessions** for short-term CBT-style work and **12–20 sessions** for medium-term therapy. Premature dropout rates across the industry run **20–57%** (meta-analysis by Swift & Greenberg). ## Partnership structure: what's legal, ethical, and optimal in Russia

**The legal environment is permissive.** Russia has no federal law specifically regulating psychologists — a proposed law was rejected by the Government Commission in June 2025. The RPO Ethics Code (adopted 2012) is **recommendatory, not legally binding**, and contains no specific prohibition on referral fees or revenue sharing. All major Russian platforms (Zigmund, Yasno, Alter) operate legally with commission-based models, establishing clear market precedent.

**Platform commission benchmarks for context:**

| Platform | Commission Taken | Psychologist Receives |
|----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Zigmund.online | ~25–36% | ~2,300–2,700 RUB of 3,590 |
| Yasno | ~30% | ~2,000 RUB of 3,150 |
| Alter | 13–15%+ | 85–87% of session price |
| Meta (bemeta.co) | 50% → 30% → 15% (declining) | Increases over time |
| BetterHelp (US) | ~50–60% | $30–70 per session |
| Talkspace (US) | ~40–50% | $21–90/hr depending on format |

**Recommended legal structures for Tim + Katya:**

Three viable approaches exist, in order of recommendation:

1. **ИП (Tim) + ИП (Katya) with agency contract (агентский договор).** Tim as agent finds clients; Katya as principal delivers sessions. Commission of 30–40% to Tim is standard and legal. This is the cleanest structure. Katya must register as ИП (not самозанятый) if revenue exceeds 2.4M RUB/year or if she wants to use an agency contract, since самозанятые cannot be principals in agency agreements involving services they render through an agent.

2. **Co-owned ООО (LLC).** Tim and Katya as co-founders of a mental health sub-brand within or adjacent to Botanica Club. Both are owners; Katya provides expertise, Tim provides marketing/distribution. Revenue is distributed as dividends or salary. Best for scaling beyond Katya to multiple psychologists.

3. **Service agreement (договор оказания услуг).** Tim's entity provides marketing services to Katya's practice for a fixed monthly fee or percentage. Simpler but less aligned on growth incentives.

**Самозанятый (self-employed) limitations:** The 2.4M RUB/year income cap (~$26,000) will be exceeded in **every scenario above** within the first 5–6 months of full operation. Katya should register as ИП from the start to avoid forced re-registration mid-year. The 6% simplified tax (УСН доходы) rate is efficient.

**The "producer" model is well-established in Russian infobusiness:** Revenue splits of 50/50 or 60/40 (expert/producer) for courses and info-products are standard. For ongoing 1:1 session referrals, a 60/40 or 70/30 split (Katya/Tim) is more appropriate given the practitioner's time investment.

## The Russian tech segment: 1 million IT workers, zero dedicated solutions

**Russia has approximately 1–1.12 million IT specialists** as of 2024–2025, growing 13% year-over-year. Average monthly IT salary exceeds **176,000 RUB** ($1,900+), and the median in 2025 surpassed **190,000 RUB**. Moscow and St. Petersburg account for roughly half of all IT professionals.

Research from HSE (НИУ ВШЭ) published in "Организационная психология" found that **68% of Russian programmers score in the high or extreme burnout range** on standardized measures, with only 5% showing low burnout. A 2024 Habr survey of 2,000 IT specialists using the Maslach Burnout Inventory confirmed burnout as a critical, pervasive issue.

**Willingness-to-pay signals are strong.** At average IT salaries of 176,000+ RUB/month, a session price of 6,000–8,000 RUB represents 3.4–4.6% of monthly income. International benchmarks suggest tech professionals allocate **5–10% of income** to self-improvement. JMIR research found moderate-to-high willingness to pay $100/month for therapy among higher-income respondents. Over 40% of IT professionals in India expressed willingness to pay for specialized mental health apps in market validation studies.

**The addressable market math:** If just **5% of Russia's 1 million IT workers** (50,000 people) sought therapy at 6,000 RUB/session averaging 10 sessions, the segment represents **3 billion RUB (~$33M) annually** in Russia alone. The global addressable market for tech-professional mental health — taking the same 5% penetration of 36.5 million developers — exceeds **$1.8 billion**.

The competitive vacuum is striking. Individual psychologists blog about IT burnout on Habr and vc.ru. Alter attracts some IT clients through its co-founder's JetBrains connection. But no Russian platform markets itself as "the mental health solution for tech professionals." Tim's Botanica Club, already embedded in the tech/crypto community, provides a natural distribution channel directly into this underserved niche.

## Innovative models worth integrating into the Tim + Katya product

Several global innovations map directly to the partnership's strengths:
